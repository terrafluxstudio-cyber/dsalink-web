Kranji Secondary School — commonly known as KSS — is a government co-educational school located on Choa Chu Kang Street 51 in the Choa Chu Kang area of north-western Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in design and innovation.

Kranji offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its design-innovation and robotics focus, its humanities and band culture, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Kranji Secondary distinctive

Kranji has a clear design-and-innovation identity. Its Applied Learning Programme, Design Innovation for Sustainable Living, runs design thinking and making through the curriculum, and its robotics culture gives that a competitive outlet — a distinctive draw for a child who likes to build and solve problems. For DSA-Sec families, the school's strengths span that applied-technology focus, a concert band and humanities culture, and a football, basketball and floorball tradition, so sporting and creative applicants both have a home. The fit is a child whose talent is hands-on and design-led, a team-sport player, or a musician, rather than someone seeking a purely academic-elite setting. Sitting in Choa Chu Kang, Kranji serves north-western families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Kranji offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Kranji is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Kranji official website](https://www.kranjisec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Kranji runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in design innovation for sustainable living, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Kranji Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Kranji Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Kranji offer IP?

No. Kranji offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Where is Kranji Secondary School located?

Despite its name, Kranji Secondary School is located in the Choa Chu Kang area (Choa Chu Kang Street 51), in north-western Singapore.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Kranji?

Kranji is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Kranji offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Kranji participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or applied-technology talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Kranji?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Kranji if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
