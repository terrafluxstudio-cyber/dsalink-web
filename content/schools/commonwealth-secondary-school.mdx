Commonwealth Secondary School — commonly known as CWSS — is a government autonomous co-educational school located on West Coast Road in the western part of Singapore. As an autonomous school, Commonwealth has greater flexibility over curriculum enrichment and co-curricular programming than standard government schools, within MOE's national framework.

Commonwealth offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strengths in STEM and applied learning, an active digital and coding culture, and the Language Elective Programme (Tamil), one of MOE's national language elective programmes.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Commonwealth offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Commonwealth is a mainstream autonomous school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Commonwealth official website](https://www.commonwealthsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Language Elective Programme (Tamil) and STEM

Commonwealth offers the Language Elective Programme (Tamil), an MOE-designated programme for students with strong Tamil language ability — one of a small number of schools offering this pathway. The school also has an established STEM and applied learning focus, reflected in its coding, environmental science, and digital programmes.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Commonwealth co-educational?

Yes. Commonwealth Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Commonwealth offer IP?

No. Commonwealth offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Commonwealth?

Commonwealth is an autonomous school competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Commonwealth offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Commonwealth participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, language (Tamil), and academic or STEM talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What is the Tamil Language Elective Programme at Commonwealth?

The Language Elective Programme (Tamil) is an MOE-designated programme for students with strong Tamil language ability and interest in Tamil literature and culture. It is offered at a small number of secondary schools. A Tamil language talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec — check the [Commonwealth DSA admissions page](https://www.commonwealthsec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Commonwealth?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Commonwealth if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
