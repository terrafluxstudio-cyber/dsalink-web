Anderson Secondary School — commonly known as AndSS — is a government autonomous co-educational school located in Ang Mo Kio in the northern part of Singapore. As an autonomous school, Anderson has greater flexibility over curriculum enrichment and co-curricular programming than standard government schools, within MOE's national framework.

Anderson offers the mainstream secondary tracks: the Express (O-Level) track, the Normal (Academic) track, and the Normal (Technical) track, with full Subject-Based Banding. It is not an Integrated Programme school. Anderson is known for its strong band and sports culture and for the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS), one of MOE's national language elective programmes.

## What makes Anderson distinctive

Anderson Secondary is an autonomous school with wider curricular and co-curricular flexibility than standard government schools. Its identity blends a strong band-and-sports culture with the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS), an MOE national language pathway. For DSA-Sec families, that draws talent in Malay language and culture, a concert band and Chinese dance, competitive wushu, and team sports including basketball, netball, table tennis and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a language-and-culture strength, a musical or dance talent, a martial-arts ability, or a team-sport strength. Sitting in Ang Mo Kio, Anderson serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

Anderson offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Anderson is a mainstream autonomous school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Anderson official website](https://www.andersonsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Elective Programme in Malay Language (EMAS)

Anderson offers the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS), an MOE-designated language programme for students with strong ability and interest in Malay language and literature. EMAS is offered at a small number of secondary schools in Singapore.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Anderson Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Anderson Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Anderson offer IP?

No. Anderson offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Anderson?

Anderson is an autonomous school competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Anderson offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Anderson participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and language talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What is EMAS and is it available through DSA?

The Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS) is an MOE-designated programme for students with strong Malay language ability. The Malay language talent pathway may be available through DSA-Sec. Check the [Anderson DSA admissions page](https://www.andersonsec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Anderson?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Anderson if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
