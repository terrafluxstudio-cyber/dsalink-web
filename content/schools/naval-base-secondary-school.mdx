Naval Base Secondary School — commonly known as NBSS — is a government co-educational school located on Yishun Ring Road in the Yishun area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in the arts.

Naval Base offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its band and choral tradition, its visual arts and dance culture, and an active character-through-sports focus.

---

## What makes Naval Base distinctive

Naval Base has a clear visual-arts-and-music identity. Its Applied Learning Programme is built around art, developing visual-arts skills across the school, alongside a band and choral tradition. For DSA-Sec families, that draws talent in visual arts, music and dance — concert band, choir, Indian dance and modern dance — and the school carries an active character-through-sports culture. The best fit is a child with a visual-arts, musical or dance talent, or a sporting strength, who would value a school where the arts run through everyday learning. Sitting in Yishun, Naval Base serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Naval Base offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Naval Base is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Naval Base official website](https://www.navalbasesec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Character through Sports

Naval Base runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in art, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on character education through sports. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Naval Base Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Naval Base Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Naval Base offer IP?

No. Naval Base offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Naval Base?

Naval Base is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Naval Base offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Naval Base participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, visual arts, and sports talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Naval Base?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Naval Base if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
