East Spring Secondary School is a government co-educational school located in the Tampines area of eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a leadership-centred approach to student development.

East Spring offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its student leadership focus, its applied-learning approach, and a co-curricular culture that builds character and community across all student groups.

---

## What makes East Spring distinctive

East Spring has a clear student-leadership identity. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a health and food-science focus, while its community-youth-leadership Learning for Life Programme places leadership and character development at the centre of school life. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes a school built around developing leaders and self-directed learners across all student groups, with an applied-science strand for the scientifically curious. The best fit is a child with a genuine leadership streak, an applied-science interest, or a co-curricular talent, who would value a culture centred on character and community. Sitting in Tampines, East Spring serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list — and whether your child's strength is offered — in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder), and see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) for key dates.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

East Spring offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: East Spring is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [East Spring official website](https://www.eastspringsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

East Spring runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or applied-learning focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on student leadership and character development. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

East Spring admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

East Spring participates in DSA-Sec in leadership. As a co-educational school with a student leadership focus, East Spring's DSA tends to attract applicants with a strong record of school leadership and community service. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is East Spring Secondary co-educational?

Yes. East Spring Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does East Spring offer IP?

No. East Spring offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for East Spring?

East Spring's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does East Spring offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. East Spring participates in DSA-Sec in leadership. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at East Spring?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to East Spring if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
