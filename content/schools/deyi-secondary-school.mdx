Deyi Secondary School — commonly known as Deyi — is a government co-educational school located on Ang Mo Kio Street 42 in the northern part of Singapore. It offers the national curriculum with a focus on applied learning and student development through the arts and media.

Deyi offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its broadcast journalism and media programme, its robotics and visual arts CCAs, its marching band, and an active sports tradition.

---

## What makes Deyi distinctive

Deyi has a distinctive media-and-broadcast identity. Its Applied Learning Programme is built around broadcast journalism, developing student voice and communication across the curriculum, and the school carries a strong media, robotics and visual-arts culture. For DSA-Sec families, that draws talent in media and journalism, visual arts and a robotics-led STEM strength, alongside a marching band and competitive basketball, football, table tennis and volleyball. The strongest fit is a child with a media, performance or design talent, a STEM-and-making interest, or a team-sport strength, who would value a school where communication and the arts run through everyday learning. Sitting in Ang Mo Kio, Deyi serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

Deyi offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Deyi is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Deyi official website](https://www.deyisec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning (Broadcast Journalism) and the Arts

Deyi runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) that enhances student voice through broadcast journalism, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) that nurtures confident communicators and gracious citizens through the arts. These MOE programmes anchor the school's applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Deyi co-educational?

Yes. Deyi Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Deyi offer IP?

No. Deyi offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What is the broadcast journalism programme at Deyi?

Deyi runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in broadcast journalism, developing students' communication and media skills. A media or journalism talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec; check the [Deyi DSA admissions page](https://www.deyisec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Deyi?

Deyi is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Deyi offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Deyi participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, media, academic or robotics, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Deyi?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Deyi if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
