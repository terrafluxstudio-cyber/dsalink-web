CHIJ St. Nicholas Girls' School — commonly known as SNGS or St. Nick's — is a government-aided, autonomous, SAP (Special Assistance Plan) school for girls located in Ang Mo Kio. It is the only SAP girls' school in the CHIJ (Convent of the Holy Infant Jesus) family and one of the small number of SAP girls' schools in Singapore, offering Chinese as the first language alongside English within a Catholic mission heritage.

SNGS offers two academic tracks: the Integrated Programme (IP), a six-year pathway leading to the A-Level examinations through its partner Catholic Junior College without an O-Level exit, and a four-year O-Level track. Both tracks are open to DSA-Sec applicants.

As a SAP school with a strong bilingual and bicultural emphasis, SNGS combines rigorous Chinese language education with the values-based character development of the CHIJ tradition.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Integrated Programme (IP)

SNGS's IP is a six-year pathway in which students do not sit the O-Level examinations and instead progress to **Catholic Junior College** for the A-Level curriculum. The IP allows the school to integrate Chinese language, culture, and enrichment across the six years.

For P6 families: the IP track at SNGS is a six-year commitment to the SNGS–Catholic JC pathway. Students who exit the IP during secondary school are placed into an O-Level stream at an appropriate year. Refer to the [SNGS official website](https://www.chijstnicholasgirls.moe.edu.sg/) for current programme structure and progression criteria.

### O-Level Track

SNGS offers a four-year O-Level track culminating in the national O-Level examinations, for students who prefer a structured external examination pathway. Both tracks share the same campus and school community.

### Special Assistance Plan (SAP) and Bicultural Studies

As a SAP school, SNGS offers Chinese as the first language with a strong bilingual and bicultural emphasis. The school offers the Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP) for students with exceptional Chinese language ability, alongside the Music Elective Programme (MEP).

For context on the IP and O-Level pathways, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

SNGS admits students through the S1 posting exercise across both its IP and O-Level posting bands. The cut-off point (COP) for each track differs and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

SNGS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, academic, and bilingualism talent areas. As a girls' SAP school with strong gymnastics, racket sports, and Chinese performing arts traditions, SNGS's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths as well as leadership and bilingual language pathways. DSA offers at SNGS may be to either the IP or the O-Level track depending on the talent area — verify which track each leads to on the school's official DSA page before applying.

The specific talent areas offered change from year to year. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is SNGS only for girls?

Yes. CHIJ St. Nicholas Girls' School is a single-sex school for girls.

### Is SNGS a SAP school?

Yes. SNGS is a SAP (Special Assistance Plan) school — the only SAP girls' school in the CHIJ family. SAP schools offer Chinese as the first language alongside English, with a strong bilingual and bicultural emphasis. Students are expected to have good Chinese language proficiency.

### Does SNGS offer IP as well as O-Level?

Yes. SNGS offers both a six-year Integrated Programme (leading to A-Levels at Catholic Junior College) and a four-year O-Level track. Both share the same campus.

### What PSLE score does my daughter need for SNGS?

SNGS is competitive for both its IP and O-Level posting bands, with different COPs for each. The COP changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does SNGS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. SNGS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, academic, and bilingualism talent areas. DSA offers may be to either the IP or O-Level track. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my daughter is not offered a DSA place at SNGS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to SNGS through the IP or O-Level posting band if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank SNGS in their school choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
