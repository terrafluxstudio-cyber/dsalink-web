Bukit Merah Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Lengkok Bahru in the Bukit Merah area of central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Bukit Merah offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its basketball and floorball culture, its STEM and applied-learning focus, and an active team-sports tradition anchored by tchoukball.

---

## What makes Bukit Merah distinctive

Bukit Merah has a distinctive sporting identity anchored by tchoukball — a niche sport few schools support — alongside a strong basketball and floorball culture. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a creative-robotics and engineering focus, and its Learning for Life Programme builds character and leadership through sport. For DSA-Sec families, those sports are the clearest draw, together with a robotics-and-making STEM strength. The best fit is a child with a team-sport talent — especially in a niche game like tchoukball — or a hands-on robotics and engineering interest, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting in Bukit Merah, it serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Bukit Merah offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Bukit Merah is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Bukit Merah official website](https://www.bukitmerahsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Student Development

Bukit Merah runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on sports and student development. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and character-building beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Bukit Merah Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Bukit Merah Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Bukit Merah offer IP?

No. Bukit Merah offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Bukit Merah?

Bukit Merah's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Bukit Merah offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Bukit Merah participates in DSA-Sec across STEM, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Bukit Merah?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Bukit Merah if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
