St. Patrick's School — commonly known as SPS or Saints — is a government-aided Catholic boys' school located on East Coast Road in eastern Singapore. Founded in 1933 and run in the Lasallian (De La Salle) tradition, it combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. The school is set on a heritage seafront campus and is part of the Lasallian family of schools in Singapore.

St. Patrick's offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strong and broad sporting tradition — including distinctive sports such as cricket and golf — alongside an Applied Learning Programme in STEM and a music and performing arts focus.

## What makes St. Patrick's distinctive

St. Patrick's is a Lasallian Catholic boys' school founded in 1933, set on a heritage seafront campus on East Coast Road. Its defining feature for DSA-Sec families is an unusually broad and strong sporting tradition that includes distinctive sports rarely found in neighbourhood schools — cricket and golf — alongside football, basketball, track and field and tennis. Beyond sport, it carries a music and music-technology focus and an Applied Learning Programme in emerging technologies. The best fit is a boy with a clear sporting strength — especially in a niche sport like cricket or golf — or a musical talent, who would value a values-based Lasallian environment. Sitting in the East Coast area, a DSA offer places students into its Express and Normal tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

St. Patrick's offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: St. Patrick's is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [St. Patrick's official website](https://stpatricks.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Performing Arts

St. Patrick's offers an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with an emerging technologies focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts. These MOE programmes anchor the school's applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

St. Patrick's admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school within the Lasallian family, students from affiliated primary schools may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

St. Patrick's participates in DSA-Sec across a broad range of sports, performing arts, academic or STEM, and leadership talent areas. As a boys' school with a strong sporting tradition — including the distinctive cricket and golf programmes alongside football, floorball, and track — plus a band and STEM culture, St. Patrick's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

The specific talent areas offered change from year to year. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is St. Patrick's only for boys?

Yes. St. Patrick's School is a single-sex school for boys.

### Does St. Patrick's offer IP?

No. St. Patrick's offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What sports does St. Patrick's offer through DSA?

St. Patrick's has a broad sporting tradition. Alongside common sports like football, basketball, floorball, and track, it offers distinctive programmes such as cricket and golf. The exact DSA-Sec sports offered change each year — check the [St. Patrick's DSA admissions page](https://stpatricks.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What PSLE score does my son need for St. Patrick's?

St. Patrick's is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does St. Patrick's offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. St. Patrick's participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, academic or STEM, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my son is not offered a DSA place at St. Patrick's?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to St. Patrick's if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
