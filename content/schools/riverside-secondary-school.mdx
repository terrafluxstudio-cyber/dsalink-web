Riverside Secondary School — commonly known as RVSS — is a government co-educational school located on Woodlands Street 81 in the Woodlands area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in global citizenship.

Riverside offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is distinctive for offering the Language Elective Programme (Tamil), an MOE-designated language pathway, and is known for its robotics and media culture and an active environmental focus.

---

## What makes Riverside distinctive

Riverside has an uncommon language-and-technology identity. It is one of a small number of schools offering the Language Elective Programme (Tamil), an MOE-designated pathway, and its Applied Learning Programme develops global-citizenship thinking through critical social inquiry, alongside a robotics and media culture. For DSA-Sec families, that combination draws talent in Tamil language and culture, robotics and media, and performing arts through its choir and concert band. The best fit is a child with a language-and-culture strength, a hands-on robotics or media talent, or a musical ability, who would value a globally minded, technology-forward school. Sitting in Woodlands, Riverside serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Riverside offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Riverside is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Riverside official website](https://www.riversidesec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Language Elective Programme (Tamil) and Applied Learning

Riverside offers the Language Elective Programme (Tamil) — an MOE-designated pathway for students with a strong interest and ability in Tamil language and literature, offered at only a small number of secondary schools. The school also runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in global citizenship through critical social inquiry, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts with a digital-citizenship focus.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Riverside Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Riverside Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Riverside offer IP?

No. Riverside offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What is the Language Elective Programme (Tamil) at Riverside?

It is an MOE-designated programme for students with strong ability and interest in Tamil language and literature, offered at only a few secondary schools. For current details, refer to the [Riverside official website](https://www.riversidesec.moe.edu.sg/).

### What PSLE score does my child need for Riverside?

Riverside is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Riverside offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Riverside participates in DSA-Sec across academic or language, sports, and performing arts talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Riverside?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Riverside if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
