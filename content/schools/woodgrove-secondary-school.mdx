Woodgrove Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Woodgrove Drive in the Woodlands area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in environmental sustainability and community leadership.

Woodgrove offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its science for sustainable development focus, its community youth leadership culture, and a co-curricular programme that develops environmentally conscious and active citizens.

---

## What makes Woodgrove distinctive

Woodgrove has a clear sustainability-and-leadership identity. Its Applied Learning Programme, STEM for Sustainable Living in the Community, runs environmental science and sustainability through the curriculum, while its Learning for Life Programme develops environmentally conscious, active-citizen leaders. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes a school built around science-for-sustainability and student leadership rather than a purely academic focus. The best fit is a child drawn to environmental science, sustainability projects or a leadership role, or with a co-curricular talent who would value that values-and-community-centred culture. Sitting in Woodlands, Woodgrove serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list — and whether your child's strength is offered — in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder), and see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) for key dates.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Woodgrove offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Woodgrove is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Woodgrove official website](https://www.woodgrovesec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Sustainability

Woodgrove runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in science for sustainable development, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Woodgrove Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Woodgrove Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Woodgrove offer IP?

No. Woodgrove offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Woodgrove?

Woodgrove's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Woodgrove offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Woodgrove participates in DSA-Sec across community youth leadership and science for sustainable development. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Woodgrove?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Woodgrove if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
