School of Science and Technology, Singapore — commonly known as SST — is one of Singapore's specialised independent schools, with an applied-learning approach to science, technology, engineering, aesthetics, and mathematics (STEAM). It is a co-educational school that follows a four-year programme leading to the GCE O-Level examinations, but with a distinctive pedagogy built around hands-on, project-based, and technology-rich learning.

SST does not participate in the Secondary 1 (S1) Posting Exercise. As stated on MOE SchoolFinder, the school does not take part in the standard S1 posting process; entry is through Direct School Admission (DSA-Sec), with STEAM as the designated talent area. For P6 families whose child learns best by building, designing, and solving real problems, SST offers a different model from the mainstream academic track.

The school is located on Technology Drive in the one-north / Dover area of western Singapore — a research and innovation district near institutes of higher learning and industry — which reflects its applied, partnership-driven orientation.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Applied-Learning O-Level Programme

SST runs a four-year programme leading to the GCE O-Level examinations. What distinguishes it is the pedagogy: an applied-learning model that emphasises inquiry, design thinking, and project work across the STEAM disciplines, rather than purely examination-driven instruction.

For P6 families: SST is a mainstream-qualification school (students sit the O-Level) delivered through a specialised applied-learning approach. Refer to the [SST official website](https://www.sst.edu.sg/) for the current programme structure.

### Technology-Rich, Partnership-Driven Learning

SST integrates technology throughout its curriculum, including a one-to-one computing environment, and works with institutes of higher learning and industry partners to give students authentic, real-world learning experiences in science and technology. These partnerships are a defining feature of the school's STEAM identity.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### DSA-Sec only (STEAM)

SST does not participate in the S1 Posting Exercise. Admission is through Direct School Admission (DSA-Sec), with STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Aesthetics and Mathematics) as the designated talent area. Selection looks for aptitude and interest in applied, hands-on science and technology learning.

Because SST is accessed through DSA-Sec rather than PSLE posting, the application happens during the DSA-Sec window in the year before Secondary 1. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's offerings, the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) for key dates, and [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview) for preparation guidance.

### What if my child is not offered a place?

A child who is not offered an SST place continues in the standard S1 Posting Exercise and is posted to a mainstream secondary school based on PSLE results and school choices. Accepting a confirmed DSA offer from any school withdraws a student from S1 Posting. For results context, see [DSA Results](/dsa-results).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is SST co-educational?

Yes. School of Science and Technology, Singapore is a co-educational school.

### Does SST offer the O-Level?

Yes. SST students sit the GCE O-Level examinations after four years, but the curriculum is delivered through a specialised applied-learning, STEAM-focused approach. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### How does my child get into SST?

SST does not take part in S1 Posting. Entry is through DSA-Sec, with STEAM as the talent area. See the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) and [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview).

### What PSLE score does my child need for SST?

SST admits through talent-based DSA-Sec rather than a PSLE cut-off. Students still sit the PSLE, but selection centres on aptitude and interest in applied science and technology. For general PSLE posting data across schools, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### What happens if my child is not offered a place at SST?

Not receiving an SST offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process — the child is posted to a mainstream school as normal. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
