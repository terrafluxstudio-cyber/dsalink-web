Orchid Park Secondary School — commonly known as OPSS — is a government co-educational school located on Yishun Street 81 in the Yishun area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in the arts.

Orchid Park offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its visual arts and band culture, its community leadership focus, and an active sports tradition.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Orchid Park offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Orchid Park is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Orchid Park official website](https://www.orchidparksec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Orchid Park runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in aesthetics — its ARTopIA art programme — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. The school also offers a school-based Enhanced Art Programme as an elective. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development; the Enhanced Art Programme is school-based rather than the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Orchid Park Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Orchid Park Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Orchid Park offer IP?

No. Orchid Park offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Does Orchid Park offer the MOE Art Elective Programme?

Orchid Park runs a school-based Enhanced Art Programme and an ARTopIA aesthetics applied-learning programme. These are distinct from the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme (AEP), which is offered at a separate set of schools. A visual arts talent pathway may still be available through DSA-Sec — see the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

### What PSLE score does my child need for Orchid Park?

Orchid Park is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Orchid Park offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Orchid Park participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, visual arts, sports, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Orchid Park?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Orchid Park if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
