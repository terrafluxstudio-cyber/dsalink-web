West Spring Secondary School is a government co-educational school located in the Bukit Panjang area of north-western Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a broad co-curricular programme spanning the performing arts and team sports.

West Spring offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its choir tradition, its shooting and football culture, and a badminton programme that anchors its sports co-curriculum.

---

## What makes West Spring distinctive

West Spring Secondary has a clear sport-and-sustainability identity. The school is known for a choir tradition and a shooting, football and badminton culture, while its Applied Learning Programme advocates for sustainability. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include competitive shooting — a precision sport few schools support — football and badminton, alongside a choir and a sustainability strand. The best fit is a child with a precision-sport or team-sport talent — especially shooting — a musical ability, or a sustainability inclination, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting in Bukit Panjang, West Spring serves north-western families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

West Spring offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: West Spring is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [West Spring official website](https://www.westspringsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

West Spring runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or applied-learning focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on performing arts and student development. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is West Spring Secondary co-educational?

Yes. West Spring Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does West Spring offer IP?

No. West Spring offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for West Spring?

West Spring's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does West Spring offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. West Spring participates in DSA-Sec across choir, badminton, football, and shooting. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at West Spring?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to West Spring if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
