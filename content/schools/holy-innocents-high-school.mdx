Holy Innocents' High School — commonly known as HIHS — is a government-aided, co-educational Catholic school located on Upper Serangoon Road in the Hougang area of north-east Singapore. With a Catholic mission heritage linked to the IJ (Infant Jesus) tradition, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. HIHS is affiliated to Holy Innocents' Primary School and to Catholic Junior College.

HIHS offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its journalism and broadcasting applied-learning programme, its band and orchestra culture, and an active community leadership tradition.

## What makes Holy Innocents' distinctive

Holy Innocents' High is a co-educational Catholic school in the IJ tradition, affiliated to Holy Innocents' Primary and to Catholic Junior College. Its defining feature is a journalism-and-broadcasting Applied Learning Programme that develops critical thinkers and effective communicators, alongside a strong band and orchestra culture. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include that media-and-communications strength, a Chinese orchestra, concert band and choir, and competitive basketball and badminton. The best fit is a child with a media, music or communications talent, or a team-sport ability, who would value a values-based Catholic environment and an affiliation pathway. Sitting in Hougang, Holy Innocents' serves north-eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

HIHS offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: HIHS is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [HIHS official website](https://holyinnocentshigh.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning (Journalism and Broadcasting) and Community Leadership

HIHS offers an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in journalism and broadcasting, developing critical thinkers and effective communicators, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These MOE programmes anchor the school's applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

HIHS admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school within the Holy Innocents' family, students from Holy Innocents' Primary may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Holy Innocents' High co-educational?

Yes. Holy Innocents' High School is a co-educational school.

### Does HIHS offer IP?

No. HIHS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What is the journalism and broadcasting programme at HIHS?

HIHS runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in journalism and broadcasting, helping students develop critical thinking and communication skills. A media or journalism talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec; check the [HIHS DSA admissions page](https://holyinnocentshigh.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What PSLE score does my child need for HIHS?

HIHS is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does HIHS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. HIHS participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, media, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at HIHS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to HIHS if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
