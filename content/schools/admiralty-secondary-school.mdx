Admiralty Secondary School — commonly known as ADSS — is a government co-educational school located on Woodlands Crescent in the Woodlands area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Admiralty offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its rugby and team-sports tradition, its robotics and applied-technology focus, and an active band and uniformed-group culture.

---

## What makes Admiralty distinctive

Admiralty pairs a STEM-and-robotics focus with a strong rugby tradition. Its Applied Learning Programme, TinkerTech, sits in STEM with an emerging-technologies focus, while its robotics culture gives applied learning a competitive outlet — a distinctive draw for a child who likes to build and tinker. Its Learning for Life Programme centres on community youth leadership. For DSA-Sec families, the clearest signals are rugby and making: a rugby player will find an established team culture, and a robotics- or STEM-minded child a genuine home, alongside netball, track and field, wushu, and a concert band and guzheng ensemble. The fit is a child with a hands-on STEM strength, a team or individual sporting talent, or a musician. Sitting in Woodlands, Admiralty serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Admiralty offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Admiralty is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Admiralty official website](https://www.admiraltysec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Admiralty runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with an emerging-technologies focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Admiralty Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Admiralty Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Admiralty offer IP?

No. Admiralty offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Admiralty?

Admiralty is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Admiralty offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Admiralty participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or applied-technology talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Admiralty?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Admiralty if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
