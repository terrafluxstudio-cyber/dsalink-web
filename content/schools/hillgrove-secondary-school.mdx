Hillgrove Secondary School — commonly known as HGSS — is a government co-educational school located on Bukit Batok Street 52 in the Bukit Batok area of western Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Hillgrove offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its visual arts and band culture, its STEM focus, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Hillgrove distinctive

Hillgrove pairs a visual-arts-and-band culture with a STEM focus in the future of transportation. Its Applied Learning Programme runs that transport-and-engineering angle, while the school carries a strong visual-arts and performing-arts tradition. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include visual arts, a concert band and choir, Malay and modern dance, and competitive floorball, netball and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a visual-arts, musical or dance talent, a team-sport strength, or a STEM-and-design interest, who would value a school where the arts run alongside applied learning. Sitting in Bukit Batok, Hillgrove serves western families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Hillgrove offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Hillgrove is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Hillgrove official website](https://www.hillgrovesec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Hillgrove runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM focused on the future of transportation, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Hillgrove Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Hillgrove Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Hillgrove offer IP?

No. Hillgrove offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Hillgrove?

Hillgrove is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Hillgrove offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Hillgrove participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, visual arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Hillgrove?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Hillgrove if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
