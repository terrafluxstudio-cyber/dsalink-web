Juying Secondary School — commonly known as JYSS — is a government co-educational school located on Jurong West Street 91 in the Jurong West area of western Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in communication.

Juying offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its choral and dance culture, its communication and language focus, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Juying distinctive

Juying has a clear communication-and-performance identity. Its Applied Learning Programme, SPARK!, develops confident, empathetic communicators through oracy, while its PASSION! Learning for Life Programme nurtures self-directed, intrinsically motivated learners. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes the intake alongside a strong choral and dance culture — choir, Chinese dance and modern dance — and competitive netball and softball. The best fit is a child with a talent for words and performance — speech, drama, choir or dance — or a team-sport strength, who would value a school built around communication and self-directed learning. Sitting in Jurong West, Juying serves western families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Juying offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Juying is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Juying official website](https://www.juyingsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Juying runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) — its SPARK! programme focused on language and communication skills — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) — its PASSION! programme centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Juying Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Juying Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Juying offer IP?

No. Juying offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Juying?

Juying is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Juying offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Juying participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Juying?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Juying if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
