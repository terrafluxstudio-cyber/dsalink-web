Guangyang Secondary School — commonly known as GYSS — is a government co-educational school located on Bishan Street 12 in the Bishan area of central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Guangyang offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its basketball and cross-country tradition, its drama culture, and an active community leadership focus.

---

## What makes Guangyang distinctive

Guangyang has a clear sport-and-drama identity. The school is known for a basketball and cross-country tradition and a drama culture, while its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a sustainability focus. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include competitive basketball and cross country, a drama and performing-arts strength, and a community-leadership strand. The best fit is a child with a sporting strength — especially basketball or cross country — a drama or performing-arts talent, or a leadership inclination, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting in Bishan, Guangyang serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Guangyang offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Guangyang is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Guangyang official website](https://www.guangyangsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Guangyang runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a sustainability focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Guangyang Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Guangyang Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Guangyang offer IP?

No. Guangyang offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Guangyang?

Guangyang is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Guangyang offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Guangyang participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Guangyang?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Guangyang if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
