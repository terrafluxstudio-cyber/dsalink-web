Bedok View Secondary School — commonly known as BVSS — is a government co-educational school located on Bedok South Avenue 3 in the Bedok area of eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in the humanities.

Bedok View offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its guzheng and band culture, its global-awareness humanities focus, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Bedok View distinctive

Bedok View pairs a global-awareness humanities focus with a guzheng-and-band performing-arts culture. Its Applied Learning Programme, Global Awareness for the 21st Century, develops world-minded thinking across the curriculum, while its Good Values programme anchors character development. For DSA-Sec families, that draws performing-arts talent — guzheng ensemble, concert band, choir and modern dance — alongside competitive basketball, football, netball and track and field. The best fit is a child with a musical or performing-arts talent, a team-sport strength, or a humanities-and-global-awareness inclination. Sitting in Bedok, Bedok View serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Bedok View offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Bedok View is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Bedok View official website](https://www.bedokviewsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Bedok View runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in the humanities, focused on global awareness, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership and values. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Bedok View Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Bedok View Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Bedok View offer IP?

No. Bedok View offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Bedok View?

Bedok View is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Bedok View offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Bedok View participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Bedok View?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Bedok View if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
