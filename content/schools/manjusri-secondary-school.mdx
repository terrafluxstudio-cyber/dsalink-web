Manjusri Secondary School — commonly known as MJSS — is a government-aided co-educational school located on Ubi Avenue 1 in the Geylang / Ubi area of eastern Singapore. Founded by the Singapore Buddhist Federation, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a strong Chinese cultural heritage.

Manjusri offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its Chinese calligraphy, Chinese orchestra, and wushu traditions, its band and choral culture, and an applied-learning focus in STEM.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Manjusri offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Manjusri is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Manjusri official website](https://www.manjusrisec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Manjusri runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a cities and urban-landscapes focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

Manjusri admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school, students from its affiliated primary school may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

Manjusri participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. As a co-educational school with a strong Chinese calligraphy, Chinese orchestra, and wushu tradition alongside a band and choral culture, Manjusri's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Manjusri Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Manjusri Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Manjusri offer IP?

No. Manjusri offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Manjusri?

Manjusri is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Manjusri offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Manjusri participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Manjusri?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Manjusri if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
