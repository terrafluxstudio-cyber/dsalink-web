Dunearn Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Dunearn Road in the Bukit Timah area of central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a focus on community leadership and student involvement.

Dunearn offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its community youth leadership emphasis, its applied-learning approach, and an active co-curricular culture that develops students holistically beyond the classroom.

---

## What makes Dunearn distinctive

Dunearn Secondary has a clear community-leadership identity. Its Learning for Life Programme is built around community youth leadership — "Every Dunearnite a Community Youth Leader" — placing service and student involvement at the centre of school life, alongside an applied-learning approach that develops students holistically. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes a school built around developing leaders and well-rounded learners across all student groups rather than a purely academic focus. The best fit is a child with a genuine leadership streak, a service-minded character, or a co-curricular talent who would value that community-centred culture. Sitting in the Bukit Timah area, Dunearn serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list — and whether your child's strength is offered — in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder), and see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) for key dates.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Dunearn offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Dunearn is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Dunearn official website](https://www.dunearnsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Community Leadership

Dunearn runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or humanities focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership and character development. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

### PSLE and S1 Posting

Dunearn admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Dunearn Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Dunearn Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Dunearn offer IP?

No. Dunearn offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Dunearn?

Dunearn's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Dunearn offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Dunearn participates in DSA-Sec in community youth leadership. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Dunearn?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Dunearn if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
