Geylang Methodist School (Secondary) — commonly known as GMSS — is a government-aided, co-educational Methodist school located on Geylang East Central in eastern Singapore. With a Methodist mission heritage, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. GMSS is affiliated to Geylang Methodist School (Primary) and to Anglo-Chinese Junior College.

GMSS offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its community youth leadership programme, its dance and band culture, and an active sports tradition.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

GMSS offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: GMSS is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [GMSS official website](https://www.geylangmethodistsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Community Youth Leadership

GMSS runs a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership, an MOE programme that develops students' leadership and service through real community engagement, alongside the school's Methodist character education.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

GMSS admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school within the Geylang Methodist family, students from the affiliated primary school may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

GMSS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and leadership talent areas. As a co-educational school with a dance and band culture, competitive team sports, and a community youth leadership focus, GMSS's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

The specific talent areas offered change from year to year. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list. For key application dates, see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline). For preparation by talent area, see [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview). ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is GMSS co-educational?

Yes. Geylang Methodist School (Secondary) is a co-educational school.

### Does GMSS offer IP?

No. GMSS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Does my child get priority from Geylang Methodist primary school?

GMSS secondary is affiliated to Geylang Methodist primary, and affiliation priority may apply in the S1 posting exercise subject to MOE's rules. For DSA-Sec, admission is governed by MOE's centralised DSA framework and talent-based selection, separate from affiliation priority. Check the [GMSS admissions page](https://www.geylangmethodistsec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) for current details.

### What PSLE score does my child need for GMSS?

GMSS is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does GMSS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. GMSS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at GMSS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to GMSS if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
