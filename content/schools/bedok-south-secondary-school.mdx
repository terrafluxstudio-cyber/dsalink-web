Bedok South Secondary School — commonly known as BDS — is a government co-educational school located on Jalan Langgar Bedok in the Bedok area of eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning and outdoor-education emphasis.

Bedok South offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its swimming and outdoor-adventure tradition, its innovation focus, and an active team-sports culture.

---

## What makes Bedok South distinctive

Bedok South pairs an innovation focus with a strong outdoor-and-aquatics tradition. Its Applied Learning Programme, Project S.H.I.N.E, develops innovation and empathy through student-centred project work, while its SHAPE Learning for Life Programme builds a healthy, physically active culture. For DSA-Sec families, the clearest signals are sport and the outdoors: a swimmer or outdoor-adventure enthusiast will find an established home, alongside competitive football, netball, floorball and badminton. The fit is a child with a sporting strength — especially in swimming or outdoor pursuits — or one drawn to hands-on, innovation-led learning rather than a purely exam-focused setting. Sitting in Bedok, it serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Bedok South offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Bedok South is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Bedok South official website](https://www.bedoksouthsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Outdoor Education

Bedok South runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) — its Project S.H.I.N.E. — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on character and student development through its SHAPE@BDS framework. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Bedok South Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Bedok South Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Bedok South offer IP?

No. Bedok South offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Bedok South?

Bedok South is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Bedok South offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Bedok South participates in DSA-Sec across sports, leadership, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Bedok South?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Bedok South if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
