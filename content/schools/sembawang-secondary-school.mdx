Sembawang Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Sembawang Drive in the Sembawang area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a focus on leadership and applied learning.

Sembawang offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its student leadership emphasis, its applied-learning approach, and a co-curricular culture that develops students holistically across academic and character domains.

---

## What makes Sembawang distinctive

Sembawang Secondary has a clear student-leadership identity. The school places leadership and holistic character development at the centre of school life, alongside an applied-learning approach that develops students across academic and character domains. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes a school built around developing leaders and well-rounded students across all groups rather than a purely academic focus. The best fit is a child with a genuine leadership streak, a service-minded character, or a co-curricular talent who would value that values-and-leadership-centred culture. Sitting in Sembawang, the school serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list — and whether your child's strength is offered — in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder), and see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline) for key dates.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Sembawang offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Sembawang is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Sembawang official website](https://www.sembawangsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Sembawang runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or applied-learning focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on student leadership and community involvement. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

Sembawang admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

Sembawang participates in DSA-Sec in leadership. As a co-educational school with a student leadership focus, Sembawang's DSA tends to attract applicants with a strong record of school leadership and community service. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Sembawang Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Sembawang Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Sembawang offer IP?

No. Sembawang offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Sembawang?

Sembawang's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Sembawang offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Sembawang participates in DSA-Sec in leadership. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Sembawang?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Sembawang if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
