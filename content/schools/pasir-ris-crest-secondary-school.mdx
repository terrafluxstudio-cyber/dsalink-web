Pasir Ris Crest Secondary School — commonly known as PRCSS — is a government co-educational school located on Pasir Ris Street 41 in eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in communication.

Pasir Ris Crest offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its communication and public-speaking culture, its band and instrumental ensembles, and an active sports tradition including fencing.

---

## What makes Pasir Ris Crest distinctive

Pasir Ris Crest has a clear communication-and-performance identity. Its Applied Learning Programme is built around developing effective communicators who impact with heart and mind, while its Learning for Life Programme centres on community youth leadership. For DSA-Sec families, that shapes the intake: PRCSS is known for a strong communication and public-speaking culture, a concert band and guzheng ensemble, and an active sports tradition that includes fencing — a niche sport few schools support — alongside football, basketball and badminton. The strongest fit is a child with a talent for words and performance — debate, public speaking or music — or a sporting strength, especially in a niche area like fencing. Sitting in Pasir Ris, it serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Pasir Ris Crest offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Pasir Ris Crest is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Pasir Ris Crest official website](https://www.prcss.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Pasir Ris Crest runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in languages and communication, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Pasir Ris Crest Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Pasir Ris Crest Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Pasir Ris Crest offer IP?

No. Pasir Ris Crest offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Pasir Ris Crest?

Pasir Ris Crest is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Pasir Ris Crest offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Pasir Ris Crest participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Pasir Ris Crest?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Pasir Ris Crest if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
