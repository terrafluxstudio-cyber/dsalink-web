River Valley High School — commonly known as RVHS — is a government-aided, autonomous, SAP (Special Assistance Plan) school located in the Boon Lay area of western Singapore. It is one of Singapore's SAP secondary schools, offering Chinese as the first language alongside English within a bilingual, bicultural educational framework.

RVHS offers only the Integrated Programme (IP): all students who enter in Secondary 1 through internal admission or DSA-Sec are on the IP pathway, which leads to the GCE A-Level examinations at the end of the six-year programme. There is no O-Level track for students entering at Secondary 1. Students who complete the O-Level examinations through other schools may join RVHS at Year 5 through the Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE).

The school's Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP) is a nationally designated MOE programme for students with exceptional Chinese language ability and an interest in deepening their understanding of Chinese culture and civilisation alongside their mainstream IP curriculum.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Integrated Programme (IP · A-Level)

RVHS runs a six-year IP culminating in the GCE A-Level examinations. Students who enter through DSA-Sec or S1 posting at Secondary 1 complete the full six-year programme at RVHS without sitting the O-Level examinations. The IP allows RVHS to design a curriculum that integrates Chinese language, culture, and bilingual learning throughout the six years within MOE's national framework.

For P6 families: RVHS is IP-only at entry. All DSA-Sec offers and S1 posting places are into the IP track. Students who exit the IP during secondary school are placed into an O-Level stream elsewhere at an appropriate year. Refer to the [RVHS official website](https://www.rivervalleyhigh.moe.edu.sg/) for current programme structure and progression criteria.

### Special Assistance Plan (SAP)

RVHS is a SAP school, meaning it offers Chinese as the first language and English as the second language, with a strong emphasis on bilingual and bicultural development. SAP schools admit students with strong Chinese language aptitude and are structured to nurture bilingual proficiency alongside academic rigour.

### Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP)

The Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP) at RVHS is an MOE-designated programme for students with a deep interest in and aptitude for Chinese language and culture. BSP students engage in an enriched curriculum covering Chinese civilisation, cultural studies, and bilingual academic work. BSP is a talent area available through DSA-Sec at RVHS.

For context on the IP pathway, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

RVHS admits students through the S1 posting exercise under the IP posting category. The school is competitive by PSLE score for the IP posting band. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

RVHS participates in DSA-Sec with talent areas across Sports, Performing Arts, STEM, Leadership, and the Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP). DSA talent areas at RVHS include:

**Sports:** Badminton, Basketball, Floorball, Netball, Softball, Table Tennis, Volleyball, Wushu  
**Performing Arts:** Chinese Orchestra, Concert Band, Chinese Dance, Modern Dance, Choir  
**Academic / Leadership:** Scientific Investigation, Robotics, and Leadership (for students with significant leadership roles in primary school)  
**Language / Culture:** Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP)

All DSA-Sec offers at RVHS are into the IP track — RVHS does not have an O-Level entry at Secondary 1.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is RVHS a boys-only or girls-only school?

No. RVHS is co-educational.

### Does RVHS offer O-Level as well as IP?

RVHS is IP-only for students entering at Secondary 1. There is no O-Level track for internal students. Students who have completed O-Level at another school may join RVHS at Year 5 through the Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE) for the final two years of the programme.

### What PSLE score does my child need for RVHS?

RVHS is competitive for the IP posting band. The COP changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does RVHS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. RVHS participates in DSA-Sec across Sports, Performing Arts, STEM, Leadership, and the Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP). All DSA offers are into the IP track. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list.

### What is the BSP and how does DSA for BSP work at RVHS?

The Bicultural Studies Programme (BSP) is an MOE-designated programme for students with exceptional Chinese language ability and interest in Chinese culture and civilisation. DSA-Sec applicants for the BSP talent area at RVHS are typically assessed on Chinese language proficiency and motivation. Read the RVHS DSA admissions page for the specific year's selection criteria. For general preparation guidance, see [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview).

### Is RVHS a SAP school and what does that mean for my child?

Yes. SAP (Special Assistance Plan) schools offer Chinese as the first language and English as the second language, in contrast to mainstream schools where English is the first language. Students at SAP schools are expected to have strong Chinese language ability. DSA-Sec applicants for RVHS should be comfortable with a bilingual, Chinese-culture-emphasised school environment.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at RVHS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to RVHS through the IP posting band if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank RVHS in their school choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
