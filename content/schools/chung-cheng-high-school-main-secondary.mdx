Chung Cheng High School (Main) — commonly known as CCHM — is a government-aided, autonomous, SAP (Special Assistance Plan) co-educational school located on Goodman Road in the Mountbatten / Marine Parade area of eastern Singapore. Founded in 1939, it is one of Singapore's heritage Chinese-medium schools, now a bilingual SAP school offering Chinese as the first language alongside English.

CCHM offers the O-Level Express track; all students sit the GCE O-Level examinations at the end of Secondary 4. The school does not offer an Integrated Programme. CCHM is affiliated with Nanyang Junior College and is known for its scenic lakeside campus, its strong Chinese language and cultural programmes, and a long tradition in the arts.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### O-Level Express (4-year)

CCHM admits students into the four-year O-Level Express track, culminating in the Singapore-Cambridge O-Level examinations. As a SAP school, Chinese is offered at a high level alongside English, with the expectation that students maintain proficiency in both languages.

For P6 families: CCHM does not offer an IP track. Families seeking a SAP school with an IP pathway may consider Dunman High or River Valley High. Refer to the [CCHM official website](https://www.chungchenghighmain.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Special Assistance Plan (SAP) and Language Programmes

As a SAP school and a centre with strong Chinese language heritage, CCHM offers an Enhanced Music Programme and the Language Elective Programme (Chinese), alongside a Malay (Special Programme) for promising students. These programmes support bilingual and bicultural development beyond the mainstream curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Campus and Facilities

Chung Cheng High School (Main) is located at **50 Goodman Road, Singapore 439012** in the Mountbatten / Marine Parade area, known for its heritage lakeside campus.

**Nearest MRT:**
- **Mountbatten** (Circle Line CC7) — approximately a 2-minute walk

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

CCHM admits students through the S1 posting exercise under the SAP category. The COP reflects the school's competitive position for the O-Level Express posting band and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

CCHM participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and Chinese language talent areas. As a SAP school with a strong Chinese performing arts and wushu tradition alongside competitive racket and team sports, CCHM's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths as well as the Chinese language pathway. All DSA-Sec offers at CCHM are into the O-Level Express track.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Chung Cheng High (Main) co-educational?

Yes. CCHM is a co-educational school.

### Does CCHM offer IP or only O-Level?

CCHM offers only the O-Level Express track. All students sit the GCE O-Level examinations at the end of Secondary 4. CCHM does not offer an Integrated Programme.

### Is CCHM a SAP school?

Yes. CCHM is a SAP (Special Assistance Plan) school offering Chinese as the first language alongside English, with a strong bilingual and Chinese cultural emphasis. Students are expected to have good Chinese language proficiency.

### What PSLE score does my child need for CCHM?

CCHM is a competitive SAP school, and the COP changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does CCHM offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. CCHM participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and Chinese language talent areas. All DSA offers are into the O-Level Express track. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at CCHM?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to CCHM if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank CCHM in their school choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
