Evergreen Secondary School — commonly known as ESS — is a government co-educational school located on Woodlands Street 83 in the Woodlands area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in communication.

Evergreen offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its bilingualism and language culture, its rugby and team-sports tradition, and an active outdoor learning and leadership focus.

---

## What makes Evergreen distinctive

Evergreen pairs a bilingual-language identity with a strong rugby tradition. Its Applied Learning Programme, Effective Communicators with a Heart, develops language and communication across subjects, while its Learning for Life Programme builds resilience and leadership through sports and outdoor learning. For DSA-Sec families, the clearest signals are rugby and the outdoors, alongside competitive netball, volleyball and table tennis, and a bilingual, language-and-culture strength. The fit is a child with a team-sport talent — especially rugby — a language or communication strength, or an outdoor-and-leadership inclination, rather than a purely academic one. Sitting in Woodlands, Evergreen serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Evergreen offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Evergreen is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Evergreen official website](https://www.evergreensec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Outdoor Education

Evergreen runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) focused on effective communication, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on resilience, character, and leadership through sports and outdoor learning. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Evergreen Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Evergreen Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Evergreen offer IP?

No. Evergreen offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Evergreen?

Evergreen is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Evergreen offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Evergreen participates in DSA-Sec across sports, academic or language, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Evergreen?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Evergreen if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
