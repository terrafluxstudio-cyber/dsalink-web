Yishun Secondary School — commonly known as YSS — is a government co-educational school located on Yishun Street 71 in the Yishun area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in the arts and technology.

Yishun Secondary offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its sonic-arts and media focus, its multicultural dance culture, and an active sports and student development tradition.

---

## What makes Yishun Secondary distinctive

Yishun Secondary has a distinctive sonic-arts-and-media identity. Its Applied Learning Programme in sonic arts develops music, media and technology skills across the school, alongside a multicultural dance culture. For DSA-Sec families, that draws talent in music and sound, Indian, Malay and modern dance, and competitive basketball, football, netball and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a musical, sound-and-media or dance talent, or a team-sport strength, who would value a school where the arts and technology run through everyday learning. Sitting in Yishun, Yishun Secondary serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Yishun Secondary offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Yishun Secondary is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Yishun Secondary official website](https://www.yishunsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Yishun Secondary runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in sonic arts — music, media and technology — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on empathy and compassion through the arts. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Yishun Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Yishun Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Yishun Secondary offer IP?

No. Yishun Secondary offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Yishun Secondary?

Yishun Secondary is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Yishun Secondary offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Yishun Secondary participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Yishun Secondary?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Yishun Secondary if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
