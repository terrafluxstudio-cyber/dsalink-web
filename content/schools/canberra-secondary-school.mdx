Canberra Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Canberra Road in the Sembawang area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in robotics and technology.

Canberra offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its robotics and media arts tradition, its badminton and volleyball culture, and a technology-focused applied-learning approach.

---

## What makes Canberra distinctive

Canberra has a clear robotics-and-media-arts identity. Its Applied Learning Programme in digital media for the visual arts develops creative-technology skills, alongside a strong robotics and technology culture. For DSA-Sec families, that draws a hands-on robotics and digital-media-arts strength, together with competitive badminton and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a technology-and-making talent, a digital or visual-arts strength, or a team-sport ability, who would value a technology-forward, applied-learning culture rather than a purely academic one. Sitting in Sembawang, Canberra serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Canberra offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Canberra is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Canberra official website](https://www.canberrasec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Media Arts

Canberra runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in robotics and technology, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on sports and outdoor education. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Canberra Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Canberra Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Canberra offer IP?

No. Canberra offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Canberra?

Canberra's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Canberra offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Canberra participates in DSA-Sec across STEM, sports, and arts talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Canberra?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Canberra if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
