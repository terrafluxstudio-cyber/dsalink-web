Temasek Junior College (Secondary) — commonly known as TJC — is the secondary section of Temasek Junior College's Integrated Programme, a government co-educational school located in the Tampines area of eastern Singapore. Students admitted to TJC's IP at Secondary 1 progress through six years, completing the A-Level examination at TJC.

TJC's Integrated Programme does not offer the separate O-Level (Express) or Normal tracks; all IP students follow a through-train curriculum that bypasses the O-Level examination. The school is known for its strong academic programme across languages, mathematics, and the sciences, alongside an active performing arts and sports culture.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Integrated Programme

TJC (Secondary) offers the Integrated Programme (IP), a six-year through-train curriculum from Secondary 1 to Junior College Year 2. IP students do not sit for the O-Level examination; instead, they progress directly to TJC's JC curriculum and sit for the A-Level examination at the end of Year 6.

For P6 families: admission to TJC's IP is through DSA-Sec and (where available) direct S1 posting. The IP is selective; refer to the [TJC official website](https://www.tjc.edu.sg/) for details on the secondary curriculum and entry requirements.

### Academic and Enrichment Programmes

TJC (Secondary) offers a broad academic programme across humanities, languages, mathematics, and the sciences, including the Music Elective Programme (MEP) for students with strong music backgrounds. These programmes anchor its academic rigour and student development across the IP years.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### DSA-Sec Admission

TJC (Secondary) admits Secondary 1 students through DSA-Sec across a wide range of talent areas, including Chinese Language, English Language, Leadership, Concert Band, Music Elective Programme, Mathematics, Science, Badminton, Basketball, Bowling, and Taekwondo. DSA offers place students into the IP pathway leading to TJC's JC programme.

Students who accept a DSA offer from TJC (Secondary) enter the six-year IP and are committed to the through-train pathway. They do not sit for the O-Level examination.

The specific talent areas offered change from year to year. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list. For key application dates, see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline). For preparation by talent area, see [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview). For historical results context, see [DSA Results](/dsa-results).

### PSLE and S1 Posting

TJC's IP does not admit through the general S1 posting exercise via PSLE score bands in the same way mainstream schools do. Admission is primarily through DSA-Sec. Refer to the [TJC official website](https://www.tjc.edu.sg/) and [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop) for current entry information.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is TJC (Secondary) co-educational?

Yes. TJC's Integrated Programme section is co-educational.

### Does TJC offer the O-Level track?

No. TJC (Secondary) is an IP school. Students follow a six-year through-train curriculum and sit for the A-Level examination at TJC rather than the O-Level examination at Secondary 4.

### How does my child apply for TJC's IP?

Admission is primarily through DSA-Sec. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for current talent areas offered and apply through the MOE DSA-Sec portal during the application window. For key dates, see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline).

### Does TJC (Secondary) offer MEP?

TJC (Secondary) lists Music Elective Programme as a DSA talent area. MEP is a specialist music programme; entry requirements are competitive. Refer to the [TJC official website](https://www.tjc.edu.sg/) for current MEP admission details.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at TJC?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to mainstream secondary schools through the S1 exercise.
