New Town Secondary School — commonly known as NTSS — is a government co-educational school located on Dover Road in the Queenstown / Dover area of southern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in digital communication.

New Town offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its fencing and band tradition, its visual arts and guzheng culture, and a digital-communication and leadership focus.

---

## What makes New Town distinctive

New Town Secondary has a distinctive fencing-and-arts identity. The school is known for a fencing tradition — a niche precision sport few schools support — alongside a band and guzheng culture and a visual-arts following. Its Applied Learning Programme develops digital communicators and change-makers. For DSA-Sec families, fencing is the clearest draw, together with a concert band and guzheng ensemble, visual arts, and competitive basketball. The best fit is a child with a precision-sport talent like fencing, a musical or visual-arts strength, or a digital-communication interest, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting in the Dover area, New Town serves southern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

New Town offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: New Town is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [New Town official website](https://www.newtownsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

New Town runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) focused on digital communicators and change-makers, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on leadership through its SPARK framework. The school also offers a school-based Enhanced Art Programme as an elective; this is distinct from the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme. These programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is New Town Secondary co-educational?

Yes. New Town Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does New Town offer IP?

No. New Town offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Does New Town offer the MOE Art Elective Programme?

New Town runs a school-based Enhanced Art Programme. This is distinct from the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme (AEP), which is offered at a separate set of schools. A visual arts talent pathway may still be available through DSA-Sec — see the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

### What PSLE score does my child need for New Town?

New Town is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does New Town offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. New Town participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and visual arts talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at New Town?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to New Town if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
