Woodlands Secondary School — commonly known as WDSS — is a government co-educational school located on Marsiling Road in the Woodlands area of northern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Woodlands offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its sepak takraw and team-sports tradition, its band and dance culture, and an active community leadership focus.

---

## What makes Woodlands distinctive

Woodlands has an uncommon sporting identity, anchored by a strong sepak takraw tradition that few schools match. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a health and food-science focus — Real Food, Real Science — while its Learning for Life Programme centres on community youth leadership. For DSA-Sec families, the clearest signal is sport: a sepak takraw, football or basketball player will find an established team culture, alongside a concert band and Malay dance culture. The best fit is a child with a sporting strength — especially in a niche sport like sepak takraw — a musical or dance talent, or an applied-science curiosity. Sitting in Woodlands, it serves northern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Woodlands offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Woodlands is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Woodlands official website](https://www.woodlandssec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Woodlands runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM focused on health and food science, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Woodlands Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Woodlands Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Woodlands offer IP?

No. Woodlands offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Woodlands?

Woodlands is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Woodlands offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Woodlands participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Woodlands?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Woodlands if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
