Montfort Secondary School — commonly known as MSS — is a government-aided Catholic boys' school located on Hougang Avenue 8 in the north-east of Singapore. Run in the tradition of the Montfort Brothers of St Gabriel, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. Montfort is affiliated to Montfort Junior School and to Catholic Junior College.

Montfort offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strong rugby and team-sports tradition, its design-and-technology applied-learning focus, and an active outdoor adventure and leadership culture.

---

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

Montfort offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Montfort is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Montfort official website](https://montfortsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning (STEM) and Outdoor Adventure

Montfort runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM — its "Made In Montfort" design, code, and make programme — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on student leadership through outdoor adventure and sports. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

Montfort admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school, students from Montfort Junior School may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

Montfort participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or STEM talent areas. As a boys' school with a strong rugby, football, wushu, and softball tradition alongside a band and a design-and-technology focus, Montfort's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

The specific talent areas offered change from year to year. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's full list. For key application dates, see the [DSA-Sec Timeline](/timeline). For preparation by talent area, see [DSA Interview Preparation](/dsa-interview). ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Montfort only for boys?

Yes. Montfort Secondary School is a single-sex school for boys.

### Does Montfort offer IP?

No. Montfort offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Does my son get priority from Montfort Junior School?

Montfort secondary is affiliated to Montfort Junior School, and affiliation priority may apply in the S1 posting exercise subject to MOE's rules. For DSA-Sec, admission is governed by MOE's centralised DSA framework and talent-based selection, separate from affiliation priority. Check the [Montfort admissions page](https://montfortsec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) for current details.

### What PSLE score does my son need for Montfort?

Montfort is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Montfort offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Montfort participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or STEM talent areas, with a particularly strong rugby tradition. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my son is not offered a DSA place at Montfort?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Montfort if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
