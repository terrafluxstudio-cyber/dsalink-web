Beatty Secondary School — commonly known as BSS — is a government co-educational school located on Toa Payoh North in central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Beatty offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its canoeing and team-sports tradition, its band and performing arts culture, and an active community leadership focus.

---

## What makes Beatty distinctive

Beatty has an uncommon water-sport identity, anchored by a strong canoeing tradition that few neighbourhood schools offer. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a smart-living, programming focus, while its Beatty Leaders for Life community-leadership programme builds an active-citizen culture. For DSA-Sec families, the clearest signal is sport — a canoeist, footballer or volleyball player will find an established team culture — alongside a concert band, Chinese orchestra and dance culture and competitive wushu. The best fit is a child with a sporting strength, especially a water sport like canoeing, a musical or dance talent, or a hands-on STEM interest. Sitting in Toa Payoh, Beatty serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Beatty offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Beatty is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Beatty official website](https://www.beattysec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Beatty runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM focused on cities and urban landscapes, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Beatty Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Beatty Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Beatty offer IP?

No. Beatty offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Beatty?

Beatty is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Beatty offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Beatty participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Beatty?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Beatty if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
