Jurong Secondary School — commonly known as JSS — is a government co-educational school located on Yuan Ching Road in the Jurong area of western Singapore, near Jurong Lake. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Jurong offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its STEM and applied-learning focus, its team-sports tradition, and an active community leadership culture.

---

## What makes Jurong Secondary distinctive

Jurong Secondary has a STEM-and-sport identity, set near Jurong Lake. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a cities-and-urban-landscapes focus, while its Learning for Life Programme builds character through sports and outdoor education. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include a STEM-and-applied-learning strength and competitive basketball, badminton, cross country and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a hands-on STEM interest, a team-sport or cross-country strength, or an outdoor inclination, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting near Jurong Lake, Jurong Secondary serves western families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Jurong offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Jurong is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Jurong official website](https://www.jurongsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Outdoor Education

Jurong runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a cities and urban-landscapes focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on sports and outdoor education. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Jurong Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Jurong Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Jurong offer IP?

No. Jurong offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Jurong?

Jurong is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Jurong offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Jurong participates in DSA-Sec across sports, academic, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Jurong?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Jurong if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
