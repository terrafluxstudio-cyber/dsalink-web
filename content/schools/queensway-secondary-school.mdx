Queensway Secondary School — commonly known as QSS — is a government co-educational school located on Margaret Drive in the Queenstown area of southern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Queensway offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its hockey and team-sports tradition, its band and performing arts culture, and an active community leadership focus.

---

## What makes Queensway distinctive

Queensway has a clear hockey-and-sustainability identity. The school is known for a strong hockey tradition that not every neighbourhood school offers, while its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a sustainability focus and its Learning for Life Programme develops community youth leadership. For DSA-Sec families, that makes it a strong match for a hockey or team-sport player — football, basketball and volleyball are also competitive — alongside a concert band and dance culture. The best fit is a child with a clear sporting strength, especially hockey, a musical or dance talent, or a sustainability-and-leadership inclination. Sitting in Queenstown, Queensway serves southern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Queensway offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Queensway is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Queensway official website](https://www.queenswaysec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Queensway runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a water and food sustainability focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Queensway Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Queensway Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Queensway offer IP?

No. Queensway offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Queensway?

Queensway is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Queensway offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Queensway participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Queensway?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Queensway if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
