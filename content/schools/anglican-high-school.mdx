Anglican High School — commonly known as AHS — is a government-aided, autonomous, SAP (Special Assistance Plan) co-educational school located on Upper Changi Road in eastern Singapore. With an Anglican mission heritage, it is one of Singapore's SAP schools, offering Chinese as the first language alongside English within a bilingual, bicultural framework.

Anglican High offers the mainstream Express academic track with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strong Chinese language and cultural programmes, its choral and instrumental ensembles, and an active sports and uniformed-group culture.

---

## What makes Anglican High distinctive

Anglican High is one of Singapore's SAP schools, with an Anglican mission heritage, so its identity is bilingual and bicultural: students read Chinese as a first language within a strong Chinese-language and cultural programme. It offers the Express track only. That shapes a distinctive DSA-Sec intake. Its Chinese cultural arts are central — Chinese orchestra, Chinese drama and wushu — alongside choir, concert band and sports including basketball, badminton and table tennis. The strongest fit is a child with genuine Chinese-language or Chinese-cultural-arts talent, or strength in a precision or racket sport, who would value a bilingual, bicultural environment with a values-based heritage. Sitting in eastern Singapore, a DSA offer places students into the Express track. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express Track

Anglican High offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) academic track with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths. As a SAP school, Chinese is offered at a high level, with all students reading Higher Chinese.

For P6 families: Anglican High is a mainstream SAP school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Anglican High official website](https://www.anglicanhigh.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Special Assistance Plan (SAP) and Language Elective Programme

As a SAP school, Anglican High combines bilingual and bicultural development with the mainstream curriculum, and offers the Language Elective Programme (Chinese) — an MOE-designated pathway for students with a strong interest and ability in Chinese language and literature. These programmes anchor the school's bicultural identity alongside its Anglican character development.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Anglican High co-educational?

Yes. Anglican High School is a co-educational school.

### Is Anglican High a SAP school?

Yes. Anglican High is a SAP (Special Assistance Plan) school offering Chinese as the first language alongside English. All students read Higher Chinese, and the school offers the Language Elective Programme (Chinese). Students are expected to have good Chinese language proficiency.

### Does Anglican High offer IP?

No. Anglican High offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) track. It is not an Integrated Programme school. Families seeking a SAP school with an IP pathway may consider Dunman High or River Valley High.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Anglican High?

Anglican High is a SAP school competitive for its Express posting band, with the COP changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Anglican High offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Anglican High participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or bilingualism talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Anglican High?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Anglican High if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
