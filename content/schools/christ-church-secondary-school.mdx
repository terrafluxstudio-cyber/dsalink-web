Christ Church Secondary School is a government-aided co-educational school located on Queensway in the Queenstown area of central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development grounded in its mission school heritage.

Christ Church offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its leadership development focus, its basketball tradition, and an outdoor adventure culture that anchors student character beyond the academic curriculum.

---

## What makes Christ Church distinctive

Christ Church Secondary is a co-educational mission school with a leadership-and-outdoor identity. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a health-science and technology focus, while its Learning for Life Programme develops leadership through outdoor-adventure learning. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include a strong basketball tradition and an outdoor-adventure-and-leadership strand, alongside the school's applied-health-science angle. The best fit is a child with a sporting strength like basketball, an outdoor-and-leadership inclination, or an applied-science interest, who would value a values-based mission-school heritage. Sitting in Queenstown, Christ Church serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Christ Church offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Christ Church is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Christ Church official website](https://www.christchurchsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Outdoor Education

Christ Church runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or humanities focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on outdoor education and student leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Christ Church Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Christ Church Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Christ Church offer IP?

No. Christ Church offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Christ Church?

Christ Church's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Christ Church offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Christ Church participates in DSA-Sec across leadership, sports, and outdoor adventure talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Christ Church?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Christ Church if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
