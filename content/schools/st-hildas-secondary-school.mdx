St. Hilda's Secondary School — commonly known as SHSS — is a government-aided, co-educational, Anglican school located on Tampines Street 82 in eastern Singapore. With an Anglican mission heritage, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. St. Hilda's is affiliated to St. Hilda's Primary School and to St Andrew's Junior College.

St. Hilda's offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strong hockey and team-sports tradition, its thinking-and-communication applied-learning focus, and an active outdoor education culture.

## What makes St. Hilda's distinctive

St. Hilda's Secondary is a co-educational Anglican school affiliated to St. Hilda's Primary and to St Andrew's Junior College. For DSA-Sec families, its defining strength is a strong hockey and team-sports tradition, alongside a thinking-and-communication applied-learning focus and an active outdoor-education culture. Beyond hockey, the school carries a concert band and choir and competitive floorball, netball and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a sporting strength — especially hockey — a musical talent, or a thinking-and-communication interest, who would value a values-based Anglican heritage and an affiliation pathway. Sitting in Tampines, St. Hilda's serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

St. Hilda's offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: St. Hilda's is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [St. Hilda's official website](https://www.sthildassec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Outdoor Education

St. Hilda's runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) focused on teaching thinking through English, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on developing the whole person through sports and outdoor education. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is St. Hilda's co-educational?

Yes. St. Hilda's Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does St. Hilda's offer IP?

No. St. Hilda's offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Does my child get priority from St. Hilda's primary school?

St. Hilda's secondary is affiliated to St. Hilda's primary, and affiliation priority may apply in the S1 posting exercise subject to MOE's rules. For DSA-Sec, admission is governed by MOE's centralised DSA framework and talent-based selection, separate from affiliation priority. Check the [St. Hilda's admissions page](https://www.sthildassec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) for current details.

### What PSLE score does my child need for St. Hilda's?

St. Hilda's is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does St. Hilda's offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. St. Hilda's participates in DSA-Sec across sports and performing arts talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at St. Hilda's?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to St. Hilda's if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
