Boon Lay Secondary School — commonly known as BLSS — is a government co-educational school located on Jurong West Street 65 in the Jurong West area of western Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in STEM.

Boon Lay offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its theatre and Chinese orchestra culture, its digital media focus, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Boon Lay Secondary distinctive

Boon Lay's character sits at the meeting point of performing arts and digital media. Its Applied Learning Programme runs in STEM with a sustainability focus, while its Learning for Life Programme centres on music and the performing arts — and the school is known for a strong theatre and Chinese orchestra culture. For DSA-Sec families, that performing-arts depth is the clearest draw: a child with a theatre, drama or Chinese-orchestra strength will find an established home, and the digital-media focus adds a contemporary creative angle that not every neighbourhood school offers. Boon Lay's DSA intake also spans football, basketball and netball alongside academic talent, so sporting applicants are well served too. As a mainstream Express and Normal school in Jurong West, it places DSA students into those tracks. A creatively inclined child — especially in stage or music — or a team-sport player fits the profile well. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Boon Lay offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Boon Lay is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Boon Lay official website](https://www.boonlaysec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Boon Lay runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a sustainability focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Boon Lay Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Boon Lay Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Boon Lay offer IP?

No. Boon Lay offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Boon Lay?

Boon Lay is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Boon Lay offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Boon Lay participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Boon Lay?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Boon Lay if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
