Kuo Chuan Presbyterian Secondary School — commonly known as KCPSS — is a government-aided, co-educational Presbyterian school located on Bishan Street 13 in central Singapore. With a Presbyterian mission heritage, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. KCPSS is affiliated to Kuo Chuan Presbyterian Primary School and to St Andrew's Junior College.

KCPSS offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its media literacy and applied-learning focus, its broad performing arts offering, and a strong servant-leadership culture.

---

## What makes Kuo Chuan Presbyterian distinctive

KCPSS belongs to the small group of secondary schools with a clear media-and-communications identity. Its Applied Learning Programme, PACE (Participate, Access and Analyse, Create, Evaluate), builds media-literacy skills across subjects, while its servant-leadership Learning for Life Programme shapes a community-minded culture rooted in the school's Presbyterian heritage. That focus shapes its DSA-Sec intake: KCPSS draws talent in media arts and journalism, dance and band, and competitive shooting and table tennis — so an applicant whose strength lies in storytelling, performance, or a precision sport will find a programme built to extend it. The school is also affiliated to Kuo Chuan Presbyterian Primary and to St Andrew's Junior College, giving families a longer pathway to weigh. A child who thrives on creative, communications-led learning rather than a purely academic-elite setting tends to settle in well here. Check the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for this year's confirmed talent areas before applying.

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express, Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical)

KCPSS offers all three mainstream secondary courses. Express students prepare for the GCE O-Level examinations over four years; Normal (Academic) and Normal (Technical) students follow the N-Level pathways with progression options. The school implements full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: KCPSS is a mainstream affiliated school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [KCPSS official website](https://www.kuochuanpresbyteriansec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning (Media Literacy) and Servant Leadership

KCPSS offers an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in media literacy — its PACE programme (Participate, Access/Analyse, Create, Evaluate) — and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on servant leadership in the community. These MOE programmes anchor the school's applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Admission Pathways

### PSLE and S1 Posting

KCPSS admits students through the S1 posting exercise across its Express and Normal posting bands. As an affiliated school within the Kuo Chuan Presbyterian family, students from the affiliated primary school may receive affiliation priority in posting, subject to MOE's rules. The cut-off point (COP) differs by track and by affiliation status, and changes each year. For current and historical PSLE COP data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### DSA-Sec Admission

KCPSS participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, media, sports, and leadership talent areas. As a co-educational school with a strong media arts and journalism focus, a broad dance and band culture, and competitive shooting and table tennis, KCPSS's DSA tends to attract applicants across these strengths. DSA offers place students into the school's mainstream tracks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is KCPSS co-educational?

Yes. Kuo Chuan Presbyterian Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does KCPSS offer IP?

No. KCPSS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What is the media literacy programme at KCPSS?

KCPSS runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in media literacy — its PACE programme — developing students' skills in analysing and creating media. A media arts or journalism talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec; check the [KCPSS DSA admissions page](https://www.kuochuanpresbyteriansec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What PSLE score does my child need for KCPSS?

KCPSS is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and affiliation status and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does KCPSS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. KCPSS participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, media, sports, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at KCPSS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to KCPSS if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices, with affiliation priority applying where relevant. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
