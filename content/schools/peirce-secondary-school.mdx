Peirce Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Sin Ming Road in the Bishan area of central Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a diverse programme spanning the arts, STEM, and student enrichment.

Peirce offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its concert band and Scouts tradition, its chess culture, and a STEM applied-learning focus that develops students across academic and co-curricular dimensions.

---

## What makes Peirce distinctive

Peirce Secondary has a distinctive strategy-and-outdoors identity. The school is known for a concert band and a strong Scouts tradition, alongside an international chess culture — a strategic pursuit few schools build around. Its Applied Learning Programme, AI-Driven STEM, builds digital-age skills. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include a concert band, competitive chess, and a Scouts-and-outdoors strand, alongside an applied-STEM focus. The best fit is a child with a musical talent, a strategic-thinking strength like chess, an outdoor-and-leadership inclination, or a STEM interest, rather than a purely academic setting. Sitting in Bishan, Peirce serves central-area families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Peirce offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Peirce is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Peirce official website](https://www.peircesec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Enrichment

Peirce runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on student enrichment and community involvement. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Peirce Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Peirce Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Peirce offer IP?

No. Peirce offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Peirce?

Peirce's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Peirce offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Peirce participates in DSA-Sec across Scouts, concert band, STEM, and international chess. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Peirce?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Peirce if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
