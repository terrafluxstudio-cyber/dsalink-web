Meridian Secondary School is a government co-educational school located in the Pasir Ris area of eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a distinctive programme spanning performing arts, visual arts, and team sports.

Meridian offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its Angklung and Kulintang ensemble tradition, its visual arts culture, and an active team-sports programme spanning floorball and football.

---

## What makes Meridian distinctive

Meridian has a clear aesthetics-and-arts identity. Its Applied Learning Programme is built around aesthetics, developing visual and performing-arts skills, and the school carries a distinctive Angklung and Kulintang ensemble tradition rarely found elsewhere. For DSA-Sec families, those musical ensembles and a strong visual-arts culture are genuine draws, together with competitive floorball and football. The best fit is a child with a visual-arts or musical talent — especially in a traditional ensemble — or a team-sport strength, who would value an arts-rich school. Sitting in Pasir Ris, Meridian serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Meridian offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Meridian is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Meridian official website](https://www.meridiansec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and the Arts

Meridian runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in a STEM or applied-learning focus, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on performing arts, visual arts, and student leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Meridian Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Meridian Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Meridian offer IP?

No. Meridian offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Meridian?

Meridian's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Meridian offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Meridian participates in DSA-Sec across leadership, performing arts, sports, and visual arts talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Meridian?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Meridian if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
