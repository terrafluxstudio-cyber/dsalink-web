Serangoon Secondary School is a government co-educational school located on Serangoon Avenue 4 in the Serangoon area of north-eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and an applied-learning emphasis in technology and community leadership.

Serangoon offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its programming and 3D modelling focus, its community youth leadership culture, and a technology-driven applied-learning approach that develops students for a digital future.

---

## What makes Serangoon Secondary distinctive

Serangoon Secondary has a clear technology-and-coding identity. Its Applied Learning Programme runs programming and 3D modelling, developing students for a digital future, alongside a STEM focus in the future of transportation. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include that coding-and-3D-modelling strength and an applied-technology focus, with a community-leadership strand. The best fit is a child with a hands-on coding, design-and-making, or technology talent, or a leadership inclination, who would value a technology-forward, applied-learning culture rather than a purely academic one. Sitting in Serangoon, the school serves north-eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Because talent areas vary year to year, confirm the current list in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Serangoon offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Serangoon is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Serangoon official website](https://www.serangoonsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Technology

Serangoon runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in programming, 3D modelling, and digital technology, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Serangoon Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Serangoon Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does Serangoon offer IP?

No. Serangoon offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Serangoon?

Serangoon's COP differs by track and changes each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Serangoon offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Serangoon participates in DSA-Sec across community youth leadership and programming and 3D modelling. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Serangoon?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Serangoon if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
