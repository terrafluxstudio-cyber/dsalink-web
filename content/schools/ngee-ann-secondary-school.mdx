Ngee Ann Secondary School — commonly known as NASS — is a government-aided, autonomous, co-educational school located on Tampines Street 32 in eastern Singapore. Affiliated with the Ngee Ann Kongsi, a long-standing Teochew community organisation, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. As an autonomous school, NASS has greater flexibility over curriculum enrichment and co-curricular programming than standard government schools.

NASS offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its strong visual arts culture, its coding and applied-learning focus, and an active Chinese orchestra and sports tradition.

## What makes Ngee Ann distinctive

Ngee Ann Secondary is an autonomous school affiliated with the Ngee Ann Kongsi, a long-standing Teochew community organisation. As an autonomous school it has wider flexibility than standard government schools, with a strong visual-arts culture, a coding and applied-learning focus, and an active Chinese orchestra tradition. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include visual arts, a coding-and-STEM strength, a Chinese orchestra and concert band, and competitive basketball, volleyball and wushu. The best fit is a child with a visual-arts, coding-and-making, or Chinese cultural-arts talent, or a team-sport strength. Sitting in Tampines, Ngee Ann serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

NASS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: NASS is a mainstream autonomous school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [NASS official website](https://www.ngeeannsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Art and Applied Learning

NASS runs a school-based enhanced art programme and an applied-learning focus in coding and digital skills. These are school signature programmes; the school's enhanced art programme is school-based rather than the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme. For current programme details, refer to the official website.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Ngee Ann Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Ngee Ann Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Does NASS offer IP?

No. NASS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for NASS?

NASS is an autonomous school competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does NASS offer the MOE Art Elective Programme?

NASS runs a school-based enhanced art programme. This is distinct from the MOE-designated Art Elective Programme (AEP), which is offered at a separate set of schools. If you are specifically looking for the MOE AEP, check the MOE SchoolFinder for the current list of AEP schools. A visual arts talent pathway may still be available at NASS through DSA-Sec — see the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

### Does NASS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. NASS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and academic or coding talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at NASS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to NASS if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
