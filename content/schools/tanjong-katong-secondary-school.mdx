Tanjong Katong Secondary School — commonly known as TKSS — is a government autonomous co-educational school located on Haig Road in the Katong / Marine Parade area of eastern Singapore. It is a distinct school from Tanjong Katong Girls' School; TKSS is co-educational. As an autonomous school, TKSS has greater flexibility over curriculum enrichment and co-curricular programming than standard government schools, within MOE's national framework.

TKSS offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is one of Singapore's leading centres for Malay language education, offering both the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS) and the Malay Language Elective Programme (MLEP) at the upper secondary level — a combination available at only a small number of schools nationally.

## What makes Tanjong Katong Secondary distinctive

Tanjong Katong Secondary is an autonomous co-educational school — a separate school from Tanjong Katong Girls' — and one of Singapore's leading centres for Malay language education. It offers both the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS) and the Malay Language Elective Programme (MLEP), a combination available at only a small number of schools nationally. For DSA-Sec families, that Malay language-and-culture strength is a distinctive draw, alongside a concert band and dance culture and competitive basketball, floorball, football and netball. The best fit is a child with a Malay language-and-culture talent, a musical or dance ability, or a team-sport strength, who would value a strong bilingual, culturally rich environment. As an autonomous Express and Normal school in the Katong area, a DSA offer places students into those tracks. Confirm this year's talent areas in the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder).

## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

TKSS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: TKSS is a mainstream autonomous school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [TKSS official website](https://www.tanjongkatongsec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Malay Language Programmes (EMAS and MLEP)

TKSS offers the Elective Programme in Malay Language for Secondary Schools (EMAS) at the lower secondary level and the Malay Language Elective Programme (MLEP) at the upper secondary level. These are MOE-designated programmes for students with strong Malay language ability and interest in Malay literature and culture; the upper-secondary MLEP is offered at only a few schools in Singapore. TKSS also functions as a centre supporting Art and Music Elective Programme learning for students.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Tanjong Katong Secondary School the same as Tanjong Katong Girls' School?

No. They are two separate schools. Tanjong Katong Secondary School (TKSS) is co-educational and located on Haig Road. Tanjong Katong Girls' School (TKGS) is a single-sex girls' school on Dunman Lane. They have different admission processes and DSA talent area offerings.

### Does TKSS offer IP?

No. TKSS offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for TKSS?

TKSS is an autonomous school competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### What is the Malay Language Elective Programme at TKSS?

TKSS offers both EMAS (lower secondary) and the Malay Language Elective Programme (MLEP, upper secondary), MOE-designated programmes for students with strong Malay language ability — the upper-secondary MLEP is available at only a few schools nationally. A Malay language talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec; check the [TKSS DSA admissions page](https://www.tanjongkatongsec.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### Does TKSS offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. TKSS participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, and language talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at TKSS?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to TKSS if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
