Hai Sing Catholic School — commonly known as HSCS — is a government-aided Catholic co-educational school located on Pasir Ris Drive 6 in the Pasir Ris area of eastern Singapore. With a Catholic mission heritage, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development. Hai Sing Catholic is affiliated to Catholic Junior College.

Hai Sing Catholic offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its robotics and applied-technology focus, its strong uniformed-group tradition, and an active sports and band culture.

## What makes Hai Sing Catholic distinctive

Hai Sing Catholic is a co-educational Catholic school affiliated to Catholic Junior College, with a robotics-and-leadership identity. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a cities-and-urban-landscapes focus, and the school is known for a strong robotics and applied-technology culture alongside a notable uniformed-group tradition. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include that robotics-and-making STEM strength, a concert band, and competitive floorball, bowling and badminton. The best fit is a child with a hands-on robotics or STEM talent, a musical strength, a team-sport ability, or a leadership-and-uniformed-group inclination, who would value a values-based Catholic environment. Sitting in Pasir Ris, Hai Sing serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Hai Sing Catholic offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Hai Sing Catholic is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Hai Sing Catholic official website](https://www.haisingcatholic.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Leadership

Hai Sing Catholic runs an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM focused on cities and urban landscapes, with a robotics and engineering emphasis, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These school programmes anchor its applied learning and student development alongside its Catholic character education.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Hai Sing Catholic co-educational?

Yes. Hai Sing Catholic School is a co-educational school.

### Does Hai Sing Catholic offer IP?

No. Hai Sing Catholic offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### Is Hai Sing Catholic affiliated to a junior college?

Hai Sing Catholic is affiliated to Catholic Junior College, which may offer affiliation considerations at the JC admissions stage subject to MOE's rules. For secondary admission, entry is through the standard S1 posting exercise and DSA-Sec.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Hai Sing Catholic?

Hai Sing Catholic is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Hai Sing Catholic offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Hai Sing Catholic participates in DSA-Sec across sports, academic or applied-technology, and uniformed-group talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Hai Sing Catholic?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Hai Sing Catholic if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
