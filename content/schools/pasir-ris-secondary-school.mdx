Pasir Ris Secondary School — commonly known as PRSS — is a government co-educational school located on Tampines Street 21 in the Tampines area of eastern Singapore. The school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development and a performing arts emphasis.

Pasir Ris offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its Chinese orchestra and multicultural performing arts culture, its digital media focus, and an active team-sports tradition.

---

## What makes Pasir Ris Secondary distinctive

Pasir Ris Secondary has a clear performing-arts identity, built on a Chinese orchestra and a multicultural performing-arts culture. Its Learning for Life Programme centres on music and performing arts (Cultural Fusion), while the school carries a digital-media focus. For DSA-Sec families, those performing arts are genuine draws — Chinese orchestra, choir, concert band and modern dance — together with a digital-media strand and competitive football and volleyball. The best fit is a child with a musical, dance or media talent, or a team-sport strength, who would value an arts-rich school. Sitting in Tampines, Pasir Ris Secondary serves eastern families as a mainstream Express and Normal school, so a DSA offer places students into those tracks. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Pasir Ris offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Pasir Ris is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Pasir Ris official website](https://www.pasirrissec.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Learning for Life and the Arts

Pasir Ris runs a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on music and the performing arts with a cultural-fusion focus. This school programme anchors its student development and performing arts identity beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Pasir Ris Secondary co-educational?

Yes. Pasir Ris Secondary School is a co-educational school.

### Where is Pasir Ris Secondary School located?

Despite its name, Pasir Ris Secondary School is located in the Tampines area (Tampines Street 21), near Tampines MRT, in eastern Singapore.

### Does Pasir Ris offer IP?

No. Pasir Ris offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Pasir Ris?

Pasir Ris is competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Pasir Ris offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Pasir Ris participates in DSA-Sec across performing arts, sports, and academic talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Pasir Ris?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Pasir Ris if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
