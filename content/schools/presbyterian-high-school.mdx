Presbyterian High School — commonly known as PHS — is a government-aided co-educational school located on Ang Mo Kio Avenue 6 in the northern part of Singapore, directly opposite Yio Chu Kang MRT. Founded with a Presbyterian mission heritage, the school combines the national curriculum with values-based character development.

Presbyterian offers the mainstream Express and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding; it is not an Integrated Programme school. The school is known for its Applied Learning Programme in STEM with a health and food science focus, its community youth leadership programme, and an active co-curricular culture.

## What makes Presbyterian distinctive

Presbyterian High is a co-educational mission school with a Presbyterian heritage, directly opposite Yio Chu Kang MRT. Its Applied Learning Programme sits in STEM with a health and food-science focus, alongside a band-and-ensemble culture. For DSA-Sec families, the draws include a concert band and guzheng ensemble, an applied-science strand, and competitive basketball, badminton, table tennis and volleyball. ## Programmes and Curriculum

### Express and Normal Tracks

Presbyterian offers the mainstream Express (O-Level) and Normal academic tracks with full Subject-Based Banding, allowing students to take individual subjects at higher levels based on their strengths.

For P6 families: Presbyterian is a mainstream school rather than an IP school. Track placement follows the S1 posting framework. Refer to the [Presbyterian official website](https://www.presbyterian.moe.edu.sg/) for the current academic programme structure.

### Applied Learning and Life-Readiness Programmes

Presbyterian offers an Applied Learning Programme (ALP) in STEM with a focus on health and food science, and a Learning for Life Programme (LLP) centred on community youth leadership. These MOE programmes anchor the school's applied learning and student development beyond the academic curriculum.

For context on the DSA-Sec process, see [What is DSA-Sec?](/what-is-dsa).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Presbyterian High co-educational?

Yes. Presbyterian High School is a co-educational school.

### Does Presbyterian offer IP?

No. Presbyterian offers the mainstream Express (O-Level), Normal (Academic), and Normal (Technical) tracks. It is not an Integrated Programme school.

### What PSLE score does my child need for Presbyterian?

Presbyterian is a government-aided school competitive for its Express and Normal posting bands, with the COP differing by track and changing each year. For current and historical data, see [PSLE COP historical data](/psle-cop).

### Does Presbyterian offer DSA-Sec?

Yes. Presbyterian participates in DSA-Sec across sports, performing arts, academic or STEM, and leadership talent areas. Use the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) to see the current year's offerings.

### What is the STEM Applied Learning Programme at Presbyterian?

The Applied Learning Programme (ALP) at Presbyterian focuses on STEM through health and food science, giving students hands-on, real-world application of science concepts. A STEM talent pathway may also be available through DSA-Sec — check the [Presbyterian DSA admissions page](https://www.presbyterian.moe.edu.sg/admissions/) and the [DSA-Sec School Finder](/dsa-finder) for the current year's confirmed talent areas.

### What happens if my child is not offered a DSA place at Presbyterian?

Not receiving a DSA offer does not affect the standard S1 PSLE posting process. Students can still be posted to Presbyterian if their PSLE score qualifies and they rank the school in their choices. Students who accept a DSA offer from any school are withdrawn from the S1 posting exercise entirely.
