# Secondary School Cut-off Points

This article provides the latest secondary school cut-off point data from 2023 to 2026. While the PSLE cut-off point 2025 is indicative, the current data remains a strong benchmark for assessing 2027’s cohort admission requirements.

## Understanding the PSLE Cut-Off Point for Secondary Schools

Secondary school cut-off points (COPs) are the minimum academic scores required for admission into different secondary schools in Singapore. They are determined based on a combination of students’ PSLE results and the school’s intake capacity. COPs serve as a guide for parents and students when making school choices, helping them assess which schools are within reach.

The table below shows the COPs for both Integrated Programme (IP) and Non-IP secondary schools. It also includes the secondary school COPs for 2023, 2024, 2025, and 2026 allowing you to observe trends and changes in admission scores over the years.

## What is my Secondary School Posting Group?

As of 2024, the traditional Express, Normal (Academic) (NA), and Normal (Technical) (NT) streams have been replaced by the Posting Groups system. Students are now posted into secondary school through three different Posting Groups – 1, 2, and 3, based on their PSLE cut-off point and performance, as detailed below.

This new system aims to provide greater flexibility and customisation in students’ educational pathways.

PSLE Score Range

21 – 22

23 – 24

Group 1 (G1)

26 – 3-

G1

Note: The primary purpose of these Posting Groups is to facilitate admission to secondary school and to determine the initial subject levels (G1, G2, or G3) that students will take in Secondary 1.

Throughout a student’s secondary education, they have the flexibility to adjust their subject levels. The three subject levels—G1, G2, and G3—align respectively with the former N(T), N(A), and Express standards. These levels can be customised at suitable points to best match a student’s strengths, interests, and evolving learning needs.
